Leadership

My experience with compassionate leadership made me think of the time I got laid off and how my supervisor handled it. It was tough news to deliver and hear, but I appreciated that she let me sit her office and figure out my next step. I consider her a mentor to this day, and she's a great expert on digital media marketing.

When I did the "multiplying goodness" meditation exercise as outlined in Search Inside Yourself, I felt good. Really good. I think I started smirking in the middle of the exercise. The realisation that everyone has the potential to create more good is self-reassuring.

I hesitated to do the Tonglen meditation. It requires you to focus on bad things and turn them into something good. I thought it was going to be too painful to do. It was... OK. I rather not make it a habit.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? I felt that some parts of it were tough to grasp. I didn't really feel a status threat: I'm a student. I know nothing. What status do I have? Certainty? I'm a student, I'm not certain of anything. As for autonomy, I did appreciate the opportunities to learn on my own schedule and figure out that I needed to improve upon - so that is self-assuring. As for relatedness, I kinda figure that we're all at EDA for good reason, so there's good reason to interact with everyone. As for fairness, I think this one is the one I was most concerned about: I wanted to make sure that I put in an effort to learn things on my own terms. Sometimes that resulted in looking at code early, or asking for help, and I realise others have put in time and effort. I hope I've been fair to those I've worked with in the past and respectful of peoples' time.

As for hard conversations: Those are never easy and they never get easy, I find. I've reflected on some toughies I've had in my life (Crucial Conversations is a great book). I don't feel comfortable sharing details of such talks online, but suffice it to say that preparation helps, practice helps, and going in with an even tone, calm demeanor and a goal of mutual benefit usually helps.
