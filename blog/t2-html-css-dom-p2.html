HTML, CSS and DOM Part II

In a follow up to some initial reflections on HTML, CSS and DOM:

  • What is grid-based design?: Grid-based design is the idea that elements are laid out on a webpage between rows and columns. Things don't just float around on a page... (Wait, I guess items can float with CSS). It's the idea that elements are laid out in a logical way: Top to bottom, left to right.
  • What's all the hype about responsive design?: What are you, my bosses? Responsive design is on the mind of every digital publisher. Responsive design is the ability for a website to adapt to whatever browser it is seen in. Regardless of whether you are on desktop, or a mobile device, that website you are browsing will show up and function beautifully. And with more and more users accessing the internet via mobile and Google ranking mobile-friendly websites higher in search, the importance of a responsive website cannot be understated.
  • What is semantic structure?: It's a way to organise information (elements) on a page. Big titles may require an h1, your subheads may deserve an h2, and your even smaller sub-subheads may need an h3.
