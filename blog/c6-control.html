Control [Yourself]

via GIPHY

When I program, I usually have a negative response when:

  • I don't know what I'm doing
  • I don't know how to do whatever I need to do
  • I *thought* I knew what I was doing, but I got an error message
  • When I see how much farther other people are in the sprint

I would say the trigger behind all these is the fear that I don't know what I'm doing. It brings back fear of sucking in maths class. It brings back the idea that I'm stupid. (Why do I care if someone thinks I'm stupid. Good question. Let me meditate on that.)

In chapter five of Search Inside Yourself, Meng teaches us a new method to handle disproportionate emotional reactions: the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond).

This method makes sense: Just create some distance from the emotion and the response. The method doesn't deny the emotion, but it provides a chance to determine just how much that emotion should fuel the reaction. I'm easily reactive, so it's good to stop, breathe, take a sec, reflect and respond. It's something I can put into practice as I attempt the week 6 sprint.
