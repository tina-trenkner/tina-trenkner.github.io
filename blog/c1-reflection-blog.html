On starting Dev Academy this week

When I first learned about Dev Academy, I wanted to apply within the first hour. I took a few weeks to think about it, because spending $11,000 should be a considered decision. Right? I liked the curriculum, I knew I wanted to learn JavaScript, and I wanted to plug the holes in my knowledge of kindergarten-level web development.

I looked forward to the sort of learning environment that Dev Academy offers. I didn't mind that the programme is new in Auckland, or that curriculum changes, or that there's not a "degree" on the back end. I figured if I put in the work to learn, the whole experience will be worth the effort.

After watching the DBC video, nothing really surprised me about the culture or the operations. This week, I think my worry is keeping up and potentially not learning what I need to be learning. That is scary.

I think right now, I'm that student who feels like she's the dumbest one in the room. I knew enough HTML and CSS to do my current job. I knew NOTHING about the command line. So I struggled to learn a new language, in order to get my assignments in on time. That was incredibly intimidating. But I learned enough to get the assignment done.

It felt good to get that command line assignment in, but I feel I might have cheated myself. (I haven't cheated. At least my intent has not been to cheat. I spent an hour this morning trying to figure out how to add text to an html file without going straight to GitHub and just editing the file there.)

I don't like this feeling, wondering if everyone else nailed the concept and I'm just scraping by. I fear that I'm not doing my part in giving and getting the most out of this experience. My biggest concern after week 1 is just making sure I'm making the time and the effort all worth it.
